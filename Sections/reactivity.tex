\begin{frame}{FRP: Parameters Influencing Stereochemistry}

Most vinyl polymers from free radical polymerization (FRP) exhibit \textbf{atacticity}, a random distribution of asymmetric carbons along the chain, due to the planar $sp^2$ radical center allowing random addition from either face \cite{kozuszek2025, wikipedia2024}.

Key parameters influencing stereoselectivity include:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Temperature}: Lower temperatures favor syndiotacticity via restricted rotation and steric control; high temperatures yield atactic polymers \cite{polycopie, kayik2014}.
\item \textbf{Solvent polarity}: Polar solvents enhance electrostatic interactions, promoting tactic chains \cite{polycopie, kayik2014}.
\item \textbf{Monomer substituents}: Bulky R-groups increase syndiotacticity through steric hindrance \cite{polycopie, kayik2014}.
\item \textbf{pH/electrostatic effects}: In acrylics, higher pH (COO$^-$ form) boosts syndiotacticity \cite{polycopie}.
\item \textbf{Additives}: Lewis acids/bases or tartrates induce stereocontrol via H-bonding \cite{kayik2014}.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{PE} is not concerned (no asymmetric carbons, linear structure). \textbf{PP} is strictly atactic in FRP (no stereocontrol possible) \cite{wikipedia2024}.

Secondary reactions (chain transfer) can reactivate dead chains, but minimally affect stereochemistry \cite{kozuszek2025}.

\end{frame}
